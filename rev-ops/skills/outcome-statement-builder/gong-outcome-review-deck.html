OCV-001
Win Rate via Conversation Intelligence
Stage 3 — Desired Outcome
Draft
Outcome Statement

When a CRO at a mid-market B2B SaaS company is managing a revenue organization where deal outcomes are inconsistent across reps and loss reasons are based on rep self-reporting rather than evidence, the CRO reduces revenue lost to undetected deal risk by TBD within TBD days of full call capture adoption, enabling the CRO to identify which talk tracks, objection responses, and competitive moments correlate with closed-won outcomes across the entire pipeline — not just the top performers' anecdotes — that advances win rate as a board-level revenue metric.

Business Metric
MetricWin rate (% closed-won)
SourceCRM closed-won/lost by rep cohort
BaselineTrailing 2 quarters pre-adoption
Direction↑ Higher is better
Verify at90-day + 180-day post adoption
Value Chain Rationale
Stage3 — Desired Outcome
The CRO's goal is evidence-based visibility into what drives wins and losses at scale — not recorded calls (Stage 2) — feeding into win rate as a Stage 4 board metric.
Achievement Rubric
L0
Not Achieved
Call capture adoption <70% of reps; CRM win/loss data not yet segmented by conversation signal.
Escalate adoption; no outcome measurement possible yet.
L1
Partial
Capture >70%; topics and sentiment flowing; managers running ≥2 call reviews/week; win rate trending up but below threshold.
Confirm coaching cadence; identify top/bottom rep cohorts.
L2
Achieved
Win rate improvement of TBD% from baseline sustained across 2 consecutive quarters; CRO can attribute delta to specific talk track in Gong.
Document in QBR; present attribution evidence to CEO; advance renewal.
L3
Exceeded
Win rate at TBD% threshold AND rep win rate variance between top-quartile and median reps narrowed by TBD pp.
Flag for case study; surface expansion conversation.

Outbound Pipeline Efficiency
Stage 4 — Business Goal
Draft
Outcome Statement

When a CRO is accountable for pipeline generation targets but outbound productivity per rep is inconsistent and follow-up execution degrades after multi-touch sequences beyond three contacts, the CRO increases outbound pipeline generated per rep per quarter by TBD% within TBD days of full AI Composer adoption, enabling the CRO to hold the pipeline coverage ratio without headcount increases that advances sales efficiency (pipeline per fully-loaded rep cost) as an operating metric reported to the CFO.

Business Metric
MetricPipeline per rep per quarter ($)
SourceCRM outbound pipeline ÷ active headcount
BaselineTrailing 2 quarters pre-adoption
Direction↑ Higher is better
Verify at90-day post full Composer adoption
Value Chain Rationale
Stage4 — Business Goal
Pipeline per rep cost is a CFO and board metric. This outcome connects directly to the CRO's operational efficiency mandate — not just a sales activity metric.
Achievement Rubric
L0
Not Achieved
AI Composer adoption <60%; outbound sequences not running through Gong; pipeline source attribution not configured in CRM.
Escalate to implementation; pipeline attribution setup required before measurement.
L1
Partial
Composer adoption >60%; reply rates or meeting booking rates trending positive; pipeline per rep trending up but below threshold.
Confirm sequence templates used, not bypassed; review reply rate data.
L2
Achieved
Outbound pipeline per rep increased TBD% from baseline over one full quarter; same or reduced headcount holding same coverage ratio.
Document coverage ratio before/after; present to CFO as efficiency metric.
L3
Exceeded
Pipeline per rep at TBD% threshold AND pipeline coverage ratio improved; CRO held or reduced SDR headcount while growing pipeline.
Flag as efficiency case study; expansion signal for additional workflow seats.

Business Metric
MetricLate-stage slippage rate (%)
SourceCRM: commit-stage wk10 vs. quarter-end close
BaselineAvg slippage rate trailing 4 quarters
Direction↓ Lower is better

Value Chain Rationale

Draft
Outcome Statement

When a CRO is committing to a quarterly revenue number to the CEO and board and the commit is built from manually reconciled CRM data with no signal from actual customer conversations, the CRO reduces forecast variance — the gap between the committed number and closed revenue — by TBD pp within TBD quarters of full Gong forecasting adoption, enabling the CRO to commit to a number with documented evidence rather than rep sentiment that advances revenue predictability as a metric the CFO uses in investor and board reporting.

Business Metric
MetricForecast variance (pp, commit vs. closed)
SourceCRO commit vs. actual closed-won ARR
BaselineAvg variance trailing 4 quarters
Direction↓ Lower is better
Verify atEnd of Q2 post adoption (Q1 = calibration)
Value Chain Rationale
Stage4 — Business Goal
Forecast accuracy at the CRO level is a board and investor metric. Highest-value outcome in the set — most directly tied to CRO credibility and retention economics.
Achievement Rubric
L0
Not Achieved

Escalate calibration timeline; set Q1 as calibration quarter expectation.
L1
Partial

Run both models in parallel; document which was closer to actuals each week.
L2
Achieved
Forecast variance reduced to TBD pp from baseline for 2 consecutive quarters; CRO committing the Gong-informed number.
Present variance trend to CEO and CFO; primary renewal proof point for this outcome.
L3
Exceeded
Variance at TBD threshold AND board/CFO has adopted Gong-informed forecast as planning input for headcount and budget modeling.
Executive story candidate; highest-leverage renewal and expansion narrative in the set.

Draft
Outcome Statement

When a CRO is managing a sales org where new rep ramp time exceeds TBD months and win rate variance between top-quartile and median reps is greater than TBD pp, the CRO reduces average new rep time-to-first-close by TBD% and narrows the win rate gap by TBD pp within TBD months of full coaching workflow adoption, enabling the CRO to scale what works from top performers across the entire org without relying on rep tenure or intuition that advances revenue per head as the primary sales productivity metric.

Business Metric
Metric (a)Time-to-first-close (days)
Metric (b)Win rate gap, top-quartile vs. median (pp)
SourceCRM: rep start date vs. first closed-won
Direction↓ Both lower is better

Achieved

L3
Exceeded
Both metrics at TBD thresholds AND revenue per head across the full org has increased — productivity showing in the P&L, not just the performance report.

Business Metric
Metric% of total revenue org covered by Gong

Direction↑ Higher is better
