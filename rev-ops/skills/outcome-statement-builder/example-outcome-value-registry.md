## OCV-001 — Win Rate via Conversation Intelligence

### Outcome Statement
> When a CRO at a mid-market B2B SaaS company is managing a revenue organization where deal outcomes are inconsistent across reps and loss reasons are based on rep self-reporting rather than evidence, the CRO reduces revenue lost to undetected deal risk by [TBD% — source required] within [TBD days] of full call capture adoption, enabling the CRO to identify which talk tracks, objection responses, and competitive moments correlate with closed-won outcomes across the entire pipeline — not just the top performers' anecdotes — that advances win rate as a board-level revenue metric.

### Value Chain Classification
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Stage | 3 — Desired Outcome |
| Rationale | The CRO's goal is evidence-based visibility into what drives wins and losses at scale — not recorded calls (Stage 2) — feeding into win rate as a Stage 4 business goal. |

### Business Metric
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Metric | Win rate (% of qualified opportunities closed-won) |
| Source | CRM closed-won/lost report segmented by rep cohort |
| Baseline Method | Average win rate across all reps for trailing 2 full quarters pre-adoption |
| Direction | Higher is better |
| Verification Milestone | 90-day and 180-day post full call capture adoption |

### Achievement Rubric

| L1 | Partial | Call capture >70%; platform topics and sentiment flowing; managers running ≥2 call reviews/week per rep; win rate trending up but below threshold | Win rate improving; [TBD] threshold not yet reached | Confirm coaching cadence; identify top/bottom rep cohorts |

## OCV-006 — Regulated Segment Revenue Coverage

### Outcome Statement
> When a CRO at an enterprise B2B company in a regulated industry (financial services, healthcare, legal) is blocked from deploying a revenue intelligence platform because security review or legal has flagged call recording, data residency, or access control compliance requirements, the CRO reduces time-to-deployment blocked by compliance review by [TBD weeks — source required] and eliminates the category of deal-intelligence data excluded from analysis due to regulatory risk, enabling the CRO to run the same evidence-based pipeline and coaching motions in regulated accounts that they run everywhere else that advances full revenue org coverage as a strategic capability metric.

### Value Chain Classification
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Stage | 3 — Desired Outcome |
| Rationale | For most CROs this is a constraint removal outcome — it enables the other five outcomes to operate without exception carve-outs in regulated segments. Full org coverage is the desired state. |

### Business Metric
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Metric | % of total addressable revenue org covered by platform (including regulated accounts previously excluded) |
| Source | Platform seat report vs. total licensed user count; compliance-cleared account list vs. total account list |
| Baseline Method | % of revenue org and accounts covered pre-compliance clearance; list of account types or segments previously excluded |
| Direction | Higher is better |
| Verification Milestone | 60 days post compliance clearance and deployment to regulated segment |

### Achievement Rubric
| Level | Label | Observable Evidence | Magnitude | CRO/CS Action |
|-------|-------|---------------------|-----------|---------------|
| L0 | Not Achieved | Compliance review not completed; regulated accounts still excluded; data redaction configuration not validated by legal or security | Baseline coverage % captured only | Escalate compliance review timeline; identify specific blocker (data residency, redaction, access controls) |
| L1 | Partial | Compliance review passed; deployment to regulated segment in progress; at least [TBD]% of previously excluded accounts now covered; call capture running but full workflow not yet adopted | Coverage % increasing; [TBD] full coverage threshold not yet reached | Confirm data redaction validated by legal before expanding; document compliance clearance artifact |
